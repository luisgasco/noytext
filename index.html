Instrumentation and Applied Acoustics Research Group

The Instrumentation and Applied Acoustics Research Group (I2A2 Research Group) was founded in 1997 due to the collaboration of a group of professors of the Universidad Politécnica de Madrid with a strong background in the fields of acoustics and electronic engineering. It’s a multidisciplinary group were people from the faculties of Industrial Engineering, Telecommunications Engineering and Topography work together in a collaborative environment. We work in the fields of acoustics and the application of the information and communications technologies to propose possible solutions based on our experience in these fields to some of the challenges that our society is facing. This vision drives us to work in close cooperation with Industry and Administrations in order to guarantee that the knowledge and the technology resulting from our work is transferred back to the society. Sometimes this also means that we offer added value services directly to the industry.

César Asensio is a PhD in Acoustic Engineering and a Senior Researcher at the I2A2 Research Group. He specializes in the field of Environmental Acoustics and Airport Noise. He has been nominated by the Spanish representation as technical expert to be part of the Working Group of national noise experts of the European Commission. He is member of the Technical Committee of CNOSSOS-EU, for harmonizing the methods of assessment of environmental noise in EU. He has been the Guest Editor of the special issues "Acoustics in Smart Cities 2017", "Air transport noise, 2014" and "Noise mapping, 2011" for the Scientific Journal "Applied Acoustics".
